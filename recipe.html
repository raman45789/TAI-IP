Gujiya

    Ingredients

    • For Dough:
    • 125 gms : Maida (All Purpose Flour)
    • 1/4 cup : Water
    • 1/4 tbsp (20 ml) : Oil
    • For Filling:
    • 1 : Nestlé MILKMAID Mini
    • 1/2 cup : Semolina (Suji)
    • 1/4 tsp : Elaichi (Cardamom) Powder
    • 2 tbsp : Kishmish (Raisins)
  • 2 tbsp : Kaju (Cashew Nuts)
  • 2 tbsp : Chopped Badam (Almonds)
  • 150 gms : Fresh Grated CoconutIngredients For Batter:

    • Recipe

    1. Step 1: To make the filling, roast semolina (suji) on low flame till it changes colour.
      Mix in Nestlé MILKMAID, elaichi powder, raisins, cashew nuts, almonds, and grated coconut.
      Cook on medium/low flame, stirring occasionally, till the mixture is dry. Divide into 16 portions.
    2. Step 2: To prepare the dough, pour oil into the maida. Add water and make it into a stiff dough.
      Knead the dough till pliable. Keep aside covered for 10-15 minutes.
      Knead dough once again. Divide into 16 balls.
    3. Step 3: Roll each ball into a flat round. Place one portion of the filling, fold it over and shape
      it into gujiya sealing the edges with little water.
      Deep fry in hot oil till golden brown.

Gulab Jamun

    Ingredients

    • 1 cup full-fat milk powder
    • 2 tablespoons all-purpose flour
    • 1/4 teaspoon baking powder
    • 2 tablespoons ghee (clarified butter)
    • 1/4 cup milk (approximately)
    • Oil or ghee for frying
    • For the sugar syrup:
    • 1 cup sugar
    • 1 cup water
    • 4-5 cardamom pods, lightly crushed
    • A few strands of saffron (optional)

    Recipe

    1. In a bowl, mix together milk powder, all-purpose flour, and baking soda.
    2. Add ghee and mix well to form a crumbly mixture.
    3. Slowly add milk and knead the mixture into a smooth dough. It should be soft and pliable.
    4. Divide the dough into small equal-sized balls and roll them between your palms to make smooth, crack-free balls.
    5. Heat oil or ghee in a pan over medium heat. Fry the dough balls on low to medium heat until they turn golden brown, stirring gently to ensure even cooking.
    6. While the Gulab Jamuns are frying, prepare the sugar syrup. In a separate saucepan, combine sugar and water. Bring it to a boil, stirring occasionally until the sugar dissolves completely.
    7. Add saffron strands (if using) and cardamom powder to the syrup. Simmer for 5-7 minutes until the syrup slightly thickens.
    8. Once the Gulab Jamuns are golden brown, remove them from the oil and drain excess oil on paper towels.
    9. Immediately transfer the hot Gulab Jamuns into the warm sugar syrup. Let them soak in the syrup for at least 30 minutes before serving.
    10. Serve Gulab Jamuns warm or at room temperature, garnished with chopped nuts if desired.




Kaju Katli

    Ingredients

    • 1 cup cashew nuts (kaju)
    • 1/2 cup sugar
    • 1/4 cup water
    • 1/4 teaspoon cardamom powder
    • Ghee or butter for greasing

    Recipe

    1. Grind the cashew nuts into a fine powder using a food processor or blender.
    2. In a non-stick pan, combine sugar and water. Heat the mixture over medium heat, stirring constantly until the sugar dissolves completely.
    3. Once the sugar syrup starts bubbling, add the ground cashew nut powder and cardamom powder.
    4. Continue to cook the mixture over low heat, stirring continuously to prevent sticking and burning. Cook until the mixture thickens and starts leaving the sides of the pan.
    5. Remove the pan from the heat and transfer the mixture to a greased plate or tray.
    6. Allow the mixture to cool slightly until it is comfortable to touch.
    7. Grease your hands with ghee or butter, then knead the mixture into a smooth dough.
    8. Place a parchment paper or plastic wrap over the dough and roll it out into a thin layer using a rolling pin.
    9. While the dough is still warm, cut it into diamond-shaped or square pieces using a knife or cookie cutter.
    10. Allow the Kaju Katli pieces to cool completely before serving.
